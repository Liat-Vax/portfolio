Overview

Putting clarity and efficiency first

An extensive redesign of the IEC's website that reflected a new approach, putting clarity and efficiency first. As part of the redesign process, we digitized complex internal and external processes such as registration, electricity bill payments, monitoring of power outages, and more.

The impact was immediate. Within days of launch, the number of people who registered and completed their tasks successfully exceeded everyone's expectations. Calls to the service center decreased significantly, and the transformation received praise throughout both general and professional forums.

My Role

Leading the end-to-end UX process

I led the overall redesign process from the UX point of view, taking a basic one-page concept and developing it into a full, comprehensive website. This included defining process stages, designing over 100 detailed screens, solving all edge cases, and managing a cross-functional team.

Challenges

The hard problems we solved

Challenge 01

IEC has so much more to offer

IEC's website offers effective and innovative solutions, but users need to register to benefit from them. Users perceived the site as a bureaucratic chore - pay quickly and leave. Our challenge: change that mindset and reveal the added value of signing in.

How we solved it

We changed the weight of each element to make signing in the natural, obvious solution to every task. We worked across three dimensions:

1. Integrating sign-in as part of every action on the site.

2. Redesigning the sign-in process to be more transparent and easier to complete.

3. Offering registration from multiple points throughout the site - recurring but unobtrusive.

Before
After

We also rearchitected the sign-in process itself. First, we mapped the full system - learning the intricate web of entity connections and dependencies. Then we rearranged them so the backend could do the heavy lifting, asking the user as few questions as possible.

The result: an easy, intuitive sign-in process that made users feel recognised - all we needed were a few more details to upgrade them to full digital client status.

A clean and simple registration process

Challenge 02

No one enjoys paying, so at least let it be easy

The most common reason users visited the IEC site was to pay a bill. The payment process was outdated and at odds with the new website's spirit. We had to redesign it from the ground up - knowing most payments happen on mobile.

How we solved it

We took a mobile-first approach and designed a clean, step-by-step payment flow where each screen focuses the user on exactly one task.

We researched payment flows widely, looking for the most innovative solution that still stayed within familiar conventions. We prevented mistakes by showing one question at a time, and created an intuitive connection between digital and physical by animating credit card input in real time.
