IDF Emergency Instructors

Digital management system, two complementary platforms

Mobile Desktop Built from Scratch

Overview

Building a system from scratch, for people who needed it most

The IDF Home Front Command's emergency instructors are enlisted soldiers who guide the general population through preparation for emergencies - from earthquakes to missile attacks. Before this project, they coordinated entirely through WhatsApp groups and Excel spreadsheets.

Our task was to design and build a complete management and tracking system from scratch - covering seven different user personas with multiple permission levels, and dozens of distinct tasks performed throughout the year.

Mobile

For field instructors on the move

Instructors move between training locations all day. Designed around minimalism and simplicity - values these young soldiers were already familiar with from consumer apps.

Desktop

For coordination headquarters

Headquarters has complex, multi-tasking needs. Designed around organized multitasking - enabling HQ to manage and track all field activity efficiently.

My Role

Research, dual concept, and cross-functional leadership

My main responsibility was conducting in-depth research of user needs and system requirements, then translating those findings into two separate but complementary concepts. I navigated constant technical constraints and frequently shifting client requirements throughout the process.

Design Process

How we built it

01: Research

Observing the work in its natural habitat

We visited instructors in their offices, conducted interviews, and observed their day-to-day - examining what hung on their walls, what they wrote in notebooks, how they used Excel. This revealed hidden but critical insights that users themselves didn't think to share.

02: Concept

Two platforms, one coherent system

Mobile: minimalism and simplicity for instructors on the move. Desktop: organized multitasking for HQ coordinators. Each concept served a distinct context while remaining deeply complementary.

03: Detailed Design

Dozens of screens, every edge case solved

Designed comprehensive screens for both platforms - covering all user flows, permission levels, and edge cases across seven personas.

04: Team Guidance

Leading a cross-functional team

Managed UX/UI designers and developers, guiding implementation while staying agile in response to shifting client requirements and new technical constraints.

Challenges

The hard problems we solved

Challenge 01

Only when you tidy up do you realise how messy it was

This project was part of a larger organisational change the unit was undergoing simultaneously. As a result, their work methodology changed several times mid-project - and we had to stay agile enough to absorb those changes without rebuilding from scratch each time.

How we solved it

We designed the system with intentional flexibility; wherever possible, building in room for adjustment without sacrificing a coherent work methodology. It was a careful balance between giving the client freedom and maintaining structure.

For example, when instructions shifted from one-per-day to hourly scheduling, we had already designed the calendar component to accommodate this change gracefully.

Challenge 02

Splitting personality, in the best possible way

Every feature had to work in two completely different contexts: for a young field instructor on a phone between training sessions, and for a headquarters coordinator managing the full picture from a desktop. Designing for one without breaking the other was a constant design challenge.

How we solved it

We designed both systems in parallel, thinking through each feature simultaneously in both contexts, and returning to our personas regularly to make sure the logic remained coherent across platforms.

This parallel approach also helped us spot where the two platforms needed to exchange data, and design the handoffs cleanly - ensuring field instructors and HQ coordinators were always working from the same source of truth.
