Overview

Redesigning how telehealth operations work

Amwell is a group of products in the telehealth world. Our project focused on improving the NOC (Network Operations Center) dashboard so that Amwell's B2B customers could operate and control their online telehealth services more efficiently.

We created an entirely new concept for their existing dashboard, one that fundamentally changed how telehealth representatives managed their day-to-day work. By deeply understanding their needs, we provided a much faster, clearer, and more intuitive way to work.

My Role

Research to concept to shipped product

My responsibility was to conduct research and then translate highly complex operational processes into a new system concept - balancing user needs, business requirements, technical constraints, and project challenges throughout.

Challenges

The hard problems we solved

Challenge 01

Sometimes you have to break something to make it truly great

The product owner had a detailed PRD and knew exactly what they wanted: a separate tab for each type of alert. After research, it was clear this wasn't the right solution - and we had to convince them.

How we solved it

Telehealth representatives handle four types of issues: long patient wait times, no-shows, provider availability, and busy waiting rooms. The product owner wanted each in its own tab - but we discovered these issues are deeply interconnected.

For example: a provider leaves unexpectedly (availability issue) → patients queue up (busy waiting room) → wait times grow (long wait time). Solving them separately would be redundant and confusing.

We created a diagram showing the overlap, then proposed a unified view with a weighted urgency parameter - letting administrators set priority and representatives tackle the most critical items first.

Challenge 02

Let someone take ownership here

Multiple representatives operated the telehealth center simultaneously without any efficient coordination tool. Several reps would unknowingly work on the same patient - wasting time, and worse, contacting patients multiple times about the same issue. Unprofessional and frustrating for everyone.

How we solved it

We created unique identities for each representative in the dashboard, then designed a clear ownership workflow:

When a representative clicks "Contact" on a patient, two things happen simultaneously: the patient becomes unavailable to other reps, and the rep receives the patient's phone number with pre-prepared comment options to log the interaction.

After a set time, if the representative hasn't resolved the issue, a notification prompts them to either complete the task or release the patient back to the queue - ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.
